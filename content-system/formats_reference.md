# formats reference

post formats are the structure your content takes. different
formats optimize for different signals — reach, engagement,
saves, profile clicks. rotating formats keeps the feed from
going stale and ensures you're hitting different audience
behaviors across the week.

this is the format system that runs @AunySillyMe on X.

---

## the rule

never post the same format twice in a row.

---

## formats and what they optimize for

### bold one-liner
one sentence. takes a clear position. no setup.
optimizes for: reach, shares, polarized replies

> "The loudest person is rarely ever the most dangerous."

---

### conversation starter
one question with a psychological edge.
can't be answered yes or no.
optimizes for: replies, comment volume

> "What's the version of yourself you keep almost being?"

---

### "do you ever" question
opens a confessional loop. assumes something about the
reader's experience without making them confirm it.
optimizes for: highest reply volume — post one every day

> "do you ever miss someone but not want them back?"

---

### educational / long-form
teaches something real from lived experience.
multi-paragraph. specific. ends with a question.
optimizes for: saves, dwell time, profile clicks

framework: Teach → Relate → Invite

---

### breakdown thread
step-by-step instructional thread on one specific topic.
numbered sections. built for saves and authority positioning.
optimizes for: saves, new followers, authority

use for tool walkthroughs, workflow documentation, system explanations.
see full spec in the content manual.

---

### news-reactive educational thread
triggered by an external announcement or update.
audience capability first — what they can now DO.
your personal example appears mid-thread, not as the lead.
optimizes for: saves, shares, new followers

structure: hook (quote post) → friction before → what changed →
what it unlocks → your example → bigger picture → actionable path →
stakes close

---

### vulnerable thread
opens with a specific personal truth. builds through honest
details. pivots to reader. never performed.
optimizes for: engagement, emotional connection, replies

structure: real thing → what it felt like → pivot to reader → close

---

### cryptic short post
2-5 words. no explanation. people project meaning onto it.
optimizes for: profile clicks, curiosity

> "Time to grow up"

---

### stream of consciousness
unfiltered real-time thought. deliberate repetition for rhythm.
feels like a voice note.
optimizes for: replies, warmth

---

### "i'll go first" post
share something personal first, invite the reader to match it.
breaks the vulnerability barrier — you model it, they follow.
optimizes for: replies, reciprocal disclosure

---

### good morning post
8-10am. selfie or image + small real thought + question.
optimizes for: early velocity, replies

---

### slice of life
specific real moment. a cat doing something specific beats
"my cat is so cute."
optimizes for: warmth, replies

---

### platform milestone / receipt post
a real result — payout, subscriber count, stream number.
brief honest reaction. ends with something conversational.
optimizes for: profile clicks

only post when the receipt is real. never manufactured.

---

## comment-pulling rule

every post longer than a one-liner must end with — or contain
early — a question that can't be answered yes or no.

the question should make the reader locate their own experience
in the topic. if it can be answered in one word, rewrite it.

**the 10 question templates:**
1. what's a moment that made [this] feel real to you?
2. what's one belief you had about [this] that totally fell apart?
3. when did you realize you were wrong about [this]?
4. what would the past version of you say about [this]?
5. what did [this] cost you that people don't see?
6. what's the hardest truth you've learned from [this]?
7. if you could go back and redo one thing related to [this], what?
8. what's a quiet opinion you hold about [this] but rarely say?
9. what story from your life instantly connects to [this]?
10. what would change if more people actually believed [this]?

**formats that don't need a question:**
bold one-liners, cryptic drops, direct business asks, "am i cooked?"
— the format itself is the engagement mechanic.
