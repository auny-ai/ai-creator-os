# content pillars

content pillars are the topics you post about consistently.
they're not a rigid formula — they're the areas where your
knowledge and your audience's interests overlap.

the system below is what runs @AunySillyMe. adapt it to
your own expertise and audience.

---

## why pillars matter

without pillars, content becomes reactive — you post whatever
feels right that day, with no throughline. with pillars, every
post connects to something bigger. your audience knows what
to expect from you. you know where to look for ideas.

pillars also make trend research systematic. instead of
searching "what's trending," you search "what's trending in
AI tools" or "what's trending in content strategy." the results
are relevant before you even read them.

---

## how to choose your pillars

your pillars should sit at the intersection of:
- what you know well enough to teach
- what you're actively building or experimenting with
- what your audience comes to you for

3-5 pillars is enough. more than that and your content
loses coherence.

## example pillar set (@AunySillyMe)

### professional pillars

**AI and tools** ← priority pillar
Claude, MCP, Obsidian, Grok, Suno, workflow systems, AI experiments.
real workflows, real results, real failures. not hype.

**X and content strategy**
how X works, what grows accounts, hook writing, format testing,
engagement strategy. from lived experience — not theory.

**personal branding**
bios, positioning, what makes someone unreplicable.
the gap between having an audience and having a brand.

**monetization and growth**
converting reach to revenue. pricing, services, digital products,
the gap between followers and clients.

**music and creator business**
the operational side of being an independent musician — distribution,
registration, rights, royalties. what musicians don't know they need.

### personal pillars

**emotional honesty** — the deep personal stuff. past, self-worth,
growth. highest engagement content.

**human psychology** — diagnosing why people do what they do.
one insight, full stop. no prescription.

**slice of life** — food, drives, cats, 1am thoughts. builds warmth.

**community** — the relationship with your audience. GM posts,
questions, thank yous.

## how pillars connect to your session protocol

at the start of every content session, Claude runs a trend
search across each active pillar. it finds what's currently
happening in each space — then filters results against your
voice before writing anything.

this means your content is always current, always on-pillar,
and always yours.

see [session-protocols/03_content_session.md](../session-protocols/03_content_session.md) for the
full research flow.

## building your own pillar system

1. list everything you know well enough to teach
2. list what you're actively building right now
3. list what your audience asks you about most
4. find the overlap — those are your pillars
5. pick a priority pillar — the one you post about most
6. document them in your content manual in the vault

once they're in the vault, Claude uses them as a filter
on every research search. trending ≠ relevant.
relevant = trending within your pillars.
