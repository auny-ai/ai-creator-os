# music 🎵

the music workflow in this system runs from generation to
distribution entirely through AI tools. Claude writes prompts,
Suno generates tracks, Soundboost AI masters them, and
DistroKid distributes to every streaming platform. Claude Cowork
handles the admin — registration, hyperfollow pages, streaming
links — autonomously.

## prompt writing (Claude + Suno)

Claude writes Suno prompts based on the emotional register,
energy, or themes you describe. the prompt style that works
in this system uses a layered bracket structure:

- opens with exact BPM
- frequency encoding (528Hz, 432Hz, 396Hz, 852Hz)
- ASMR texture layers — water droplets, heartbeat bass,
  flatline synths, fire clicking, sonar pings
- quality controls — no white noise, seamless transitions,
  specific instrumentation

describe what you want to feel, not just what genre you want.
Claude translates that into a structured Suno prompt.

prompt examples and structure: see [stack/suno.md](../stack/suno.md)

## mastering

after selecting your tracks from Suno, run them through
[Soundboost AI](https://soundboost.ai) for streaming-ready
mastering. upload the track, choose your loudness target,
download the master.

## distribution (DistroKid)

→ [get DistroKid](https://distrokid.com/vip/seven/11119560) — 7% off

upload your mastered track with:
- artwork (3000x3000px minimum, JPG or PNG)
- metadata — title, artist, genre, release date
- ISRC (DistroKid can generate this automatically)

timeline: live on Spotify and Apple Music within 24-72 hours.
set your release date at least 7 days out for playlist
consideration.

---

## registration (ASCAP)

register your releases with your performing rights organisation
(ASCAP in the US) to collect performance royalties.

in this system, Claude Cowork handles ASCAP registration
autonomously — it reads the release catalog from the vault
and registers each title, filling in all required fields.
a full 150-song catalog was registered this way in a single
session.

---

## storing your catalog

every release gets its own entry in the vault — streaming links,
hyperfollow page, release date, ISRC, platform status.
Claude reads this when drafting release content, building
new hyperfollow pages, or referencing catalog details.

this means Claude always has your full discography available
mid-session without you having to look anything up.
